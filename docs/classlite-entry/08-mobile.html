Chapter 8 · screens 74 – 87 Cross-role · mobile

On the phone — where the work actually happens for half the roles.

The first seven chapters were drawn at desktop because that's where teachers grade and owners run a center. But students live on their phones — Duc opens ClassLite on the bus, at his desk, in bed before checking tomorrow's deadline. Teachers use mobile for triage between sessions. Owners use it to approve an enrolment without being at a laptop. Mobile parity is not "design 73 mobile screens" — it's designing the surfaces that actually get reached for on a phone, and being explicit about which ones don't. This chapter draws the 14 screens that earn their phone slot — thirteen device-fidelity screens plus a coverage map showing what is mobile-first, mobile-triage, and desktop-only by design.

"however" vs "on the contrary"
M
Minh Nguyen
Teacher · in IELTS Writing 6.5
Your question
Teacher, in my second body paragraph, should I use "however" or "on the contrary" to introduce the opposing view?
Both work, but "however" is more natural in essay writing. "On the contrary" is stronger and used when you're directly contradicting a previous statement (e.g. "I love coffee. On the contrary, my brother hates it"). Your context is just contrast — introducing a different perspective — so go with "however".
Quick rule: use "on the contrary" when you mean "the opposite is true." Otherwise stick with "however", "nevertheless", or "in contrast" depending on rhythm.

87 / Meta · Mobile coverage map

What is & isn't mobile — by design

Every desktop screen from chapters 1–7, grouped into three buckets: Mobile-first (purpose-built mobile screen in this chapter), Mobile triage (works on a phone but not the best surface), and Desktop-only by design (not on the phone roadmap because the work doesn't fit). This makes the asymmetry explicit so reviewers don't read mobile gaps as oversights — every desktop screen sits in one of these three buckets, deliberately.
Design philosophy
Mobile parity isn't 73 phone screens. It's designing for the moments a phone actually fits, and being explicit about the rest.
The 14 mobile screens in this chapter are purpose-designed — not responsive renders. They cover where Duc lives (writing, reading feedback, asking questions, checking his trajectory), where Minh triages between sessions (inbox, quick replies, class health), and where Huong needs to act away from her desk (approving enrolments). The rest of the desktop product is intentionally not on mobile — not as a backlog, but as a design choice. Below: every desktop screen, placed.

14 screens designed for phone
