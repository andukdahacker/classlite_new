ClassLite UI Mockup · Chapter 1

Owner onboarding

Three onboarding paths shaped by who's setting the center up: Operator (admin/secretary, doesn't teach), Solo teacher (teaches alone, no center to manage), and Founder (operates a small center and also teaches). Each path drives toward its own primary action and defers the rest to a Finish-setup card on the dashboard.

Design note · vocabulary shift "Center owner" became Operator because the role is administrative (scheduling, enrolment, money flow) rather than ownership-defined. At big centers this is professional staff; at small centers it's the teacher's secretary. "Owner & teacher" became Founder because the path describes someone who started teaching and grew into running a small center. Admins invited by an operator still aren't shown here — they receive invites, they don't sign up cold.
Chapter 1 · screens 01 – 04 Operator

Path A · Trang sets up Saigon English Center

Trang Tran is the secretary at a small IELTS center — her boss owns it, she runs it. She picks Operator. Her job is plural-everything: dozens of teachers, classes, and students. So onboarding asks her to build one class template (a recipe) and then duplicate it into the real classes she's running — each with its own teacher and optional students. Class templates are the bulk unit; teacher assignment happens inline per class. Every step is skippable; whatever she doesn't finish in onboarding becomes a dashboard task.

Center setup One template (suggested or custom) Spawn N classes from the template Per-class: teacher (required) + students (optional) Every step skippable
Time on screen: ≈4–6 minutes Outcome: Center named · template built · N classes spawned with teachers

Chapter 3 · screens 07 – 08 Founder

Path C · Linh runs a small center and teaches

Linh Hoang owns a small IELTS school she started teaching in. Same flow as the operator — Center setup → Build template → Spawn classes — but the framing shifts: at least one of the spawned classes is hers to teach, so the teacher field defaults to her. She still invites other teachers per class for any she doesn't take on herself. Every step is skippable; classes she doesn't spawn now spawn from the dashboard later.

Same shape as operator Teacher defaults to founder on the first spawned class Other teachers invited per class as needed Every step skippable
Time on screen: ≈3–5 minutes Outcome: Center · template · class she'll teach · maybe more

Design note · founder path divergence Structurally identical to the operator path. The only difference is the spawn screen's defaults: the first row auto-assigns the founder as the teacher (she's already a verified user in the system as both founder and teacher). Additional rows behave the same as the operator's — empty teacher field with inline-invite affordance. The founder's Done screen would match the operator's structure, with stats reflecting "1 template · N classes (you teach N of them) · M teachers invited" — not drawn separately.
Chapter 4 · screen 09 After onboarding

Day 1 · the handoff

When the user opens the Dashboard from the Done screen, they land on the real product — sidebar, classes, the works. The classes spawned during onboarding are right there as cards. Sitting at the top of the canvas is a Finish setting up card with what's still left: enrol students, spawn more classes, set up billing. Drawn from the operator's perspective; founder's card looks similar with their own classes shown. Solo card holds a different set entirely.

Real dashboard sidebar Finish-setup card pinned to top Different task set per persona Dismissable · re-openable from Settings
Time on screen: indefinite — lives until tasks done or dismissed Outcome: User can finish setup at their pace
